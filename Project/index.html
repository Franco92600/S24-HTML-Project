1992 Nissan Skyline

Skyline GTR

The GT-R model is the top trim level available for the Skyline platform. It features the brand new RB26DETT engine, a 2.6L twin-turbocharged inline-6 producing an official 276 horsepower and 271 lb-ft of torque. Along with it's ATTESA E-TS all-wheel-drive system and Super HICAS all wheel steering, The GTR is a serious performance vehicle built to push the limit of what a sports car can truly be. The GT-R is only available as a 2-door coupe, with AWD and a 5-speed manual transmission

Skyline GTS

Available in both sedan and coupe body styles, the GTS is powered by the RB20DE engine, a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated inline-six that produces around 156 horsepower. This model features rear-wheel drive and was equipped with Nissan’s HICAS four-wheel steering system, enhancing its handling capabilities. The GTS offered a balance of performance and practicality, making it a perfect choice for enthusiasts who appreciate sporty yet refined characteristics.

Skyline GTE

Powered by the RB20E engine, a 2.0-liter naturally aspirated inline-six that produces around 123 horsepower.The GTE is available only in a sedan body style and featured rear-wheel drive. It offers a more affordable and practical option while still maintaining the Skyline’s quality and driving enjoyment.
